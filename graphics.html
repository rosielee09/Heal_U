Graphics

This document provides guidelines for maintaining visual consistency across our product.

Navigation Bar

Home

The Home button navigates users to the home screen, providing quick access to the main interface.

Diagnose

The diagnose button navigates users to the Diagnose page, allowing them to reassess their condition anytime, especially if their pain level changes significantly.

Progress

The Progress button takes users to the Progress page, where they can track their journey from the beginning to their current pain condition.

Blog

The Blog button takes users to the blog page, allowing them to interact with others and find motivation through shared experiences.

Settings

The Settings button takes users to the settings page, where they can customize their preferences and personalize their experience.

Icons

User Setting

Type of Pain

To enhance user interaction and provide a clearer visual understanding, we have created 40x40px pain type description icons. These icons are designed to visually represent different pain types (such as sharp, dull, throbbing, etc.), offering users a quick and intuitive way to associate their pain experience with an appropriate icon. The small, consistent size ensures the icons are easily recognizable across various devices without overwhelming the interface.


Pain Scale

We’ve created interactive pain scale icons for everyday user check-ins, allowing users to select their pain level easily. These icons dynamically update to show pain increase or decrease over time, offering real-time feedback. The visual representation of pain levels, coupled with intuitive icons, helps users better understand their progress. This interactive approach ensures clarity and engagement, promoting an accessible experience for all users, including those with cognitive or sensory impairments.


Illustrations

We use illustrations during the survey and onboarding process to help assess pain levels and conditions, enabling us to provide a tailored recovery plan. The visuals are placed in the background of question sections (400px by 260px) with a 24px border-radius and a tertiary background color, ensuring they stand out without distracting from the content. These high-quality illustrations are sourced from Adobe Stock.


background: 400x260 px

color: tertiary #F8F3EC

border-radius: 24px

Images for Contents

We use positive, uplifting images primarily on the Blogs page—where users share their recovery stories—and as thumbnails for tutorial videos in the recovery plan section. These visuals reflect the core message of our brand: that consistent exercise can lead to meaningful pain improvement. Each image features a border-radius:24px to convey warmth and friendliness in design. All images are sourced from Unsplash, ensuring high-quality, diverse, and royalty-free visuals that support inclusivity and accessibility.


Screens

In our app, we have intentionally designed each screen to focus on one main feature at a time. This simple, clean approach ensures that users are not overwhelmed and can easily navigate through the app without confusion. To enhance accessibility and usability, we have used clear, easily readable fonts, and incorporated distinct color schemes to differentiate sections, making it intuitive for users to distinguish between various functionalities.


Setting for Accessibility

To cater to users with diverse needs, we provide options in the app’s user settings that allow them to customize their experience. This includes the ability to switch between light and dark mode, adjust sound settings, and modify notification preferences. These settings ensure that users can personalize their experience to accommodate their visual preferences and sensory needs.


Tutorial for splash screen


A tutorial on the splash screen helps guide users through the initial experience of your app, ensuring that all users, regardless of their familiarity with the app or technical skills, can easily understand how to use it.
